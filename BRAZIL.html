It is worth mentioning that the length of the statue reaches 30 meters, and it can be clearly seen from many places in the city.

[3]Salvador

It is one of the cities in Brazil located in the northeast of the country within the state of Bahia. It is considered a historical city that hosts many tourist attractions, including picturesque beaches such as Porto de Barra and Stella Maris. The city is famous for its many festivals and carnivals, so much so that it is called the City of Happiness, as the city’s residents share their joy in these carnivals with the city’s visitors and tourists from all over the world.

Salvador top spots!

  • Porto da Barra beach

    Porto da Barra is a natural, curved beach of golden sand, located near one of the largest cities in Brazil, Salvador, and is considered one of its most important tourist facilities, which annually attracts more than two million visitors. The beach is distinguished by its charming natural composition and low wave suitable for practicing all types of sports. In 2014, the beach was listed among the 50 best beaches in the world!

    • Discover it!

  • Church and Monastery of Sao Francisco

    The Church and Monastery of São Francisco is located in the city of Salvador. It is one of the simplest and most beautiful churches in the city, dating back to the eighteenth century. This church is distinguished by its decorated walls (interior and exterior), colorful windows, and a ceiling filled with multiple religious paintings. In addition to chandeliers, statues, and a magnificent altar. Make sure to wear appropriate clothing, and go to visit this church with family or friends.

    • Discover it!

  • Bahian Carnival

    Bahian Carnival is the annual carnival festival celebrated in the Brazilian state of Bahia, mainly in its capital, Salvador. The event officially lasts for six days, beginning on the Thursday before Ash Wednesday and concluding on Ash Wednesday at noon. The term may also be used to comprise related events that happen immediately before or after, extending the duration for up to twelve days.
